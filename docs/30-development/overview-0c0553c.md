# Overview

As a partner, you can develop ABAP content on SAP BTP ABAP environment and distribute your content to your customers. This topic provides an overview of your options.



\(image\)



## Add-on Product Scenario

As a partner, you can provide an installable product that your customers can install on their systems. Your product can contain ready-to-use applications and reusable content that your customers can integrate into their development.

For a detailed step-by-step description of the process, see:

-   Developing a Product
-   Offering an installable Product



## MT SaaS Product Scenario

As a partner, you can provide a BTP subscription service to your customers. When customers subscribe, they receive access to a tenant in a multitenant ABAP system, where they can consume your business applications.

For a step-by-step description of the process, see

-   Developing an Add-on
-   Offering a Product as a SaaS Solution



### gCTS Scenario

Instead of delivering your content as an add-on, you can use gCTS to import your ABAP development into systems. When you use this approach, develop your content and follow the steps in **Offering a Product as a SaaS Solution**, with some exceptions. For a detailed description of this approach, see \[link to gCTS scenario page\].

> ### Note:  
> The Landscape Portal does not support the gCTS scenario.



## Landscape Portal

The Landscape Portal helps you perform administration tasks, manage lifecycle operations, and configure multitenant SaaS solutions. Administration tasks include hibernating systems, creating test tenants and support users, and selecting systems for pre-upgrade. Lifecycle operations include building, deploying, and updating product versions. You can also configure and deploy multitenant SaaS solutions.
